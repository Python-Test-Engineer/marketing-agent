# Brand Voice and Messaging

Brand voice is *how* you say things; messaging is *what* you say. Together they create a consistent, recognizable brand in every piece of content. See [[content_strategy_overview|Content Strategy Overview]] for how this fits the larger picture.

## Voice vs. Tone

| | Voice | Tone |
|-|-------|------|
| **Definition** | Consistent personality | Situational adjustment |
| **Changes?** | Never | Always |
| **Example** | "We're direct and empathetic" | Serious in a crisis, playful in a win |

## Voice Attribute Framework

Define 3–4 voice attributes, each with:
- **What it is**
- **What it is NOT**
- **Example in copy**

```
Attribute: Clear
✅ Plain language, no jargon
❌ Not dumbed-down or condescending
Example: "Here's what happened and why it matters."
```

## Messaging Architecture

```
Company Tagline
    └── Value Proposition (for all audiences)
            ├── Persona 1 Message → [[audience_research_and_personas|Audience Research and Personas]]
            ├── Persona 2 Message
            └── Persona 3 Message
```

## Core Messaging Pillars

Typically 3–5 pillars that every content piece should touch on at least one of:

1. **Transformation** — What changes for the customer?
2. **Differentiation** — Why you over alternatives?
3. **Proof** — Evidence that backs your claims
4. **Urgency** — Why act now?
5. **Belonging** — Community/identity alignment

## Copy Checklist

- [ ] Does this reflect our voice attributes?
- [ ] Does it address a specific [[audience_research_and_personas|persona]]?
- [ ] Is the CTA clear and singular?
- [ ] Does it connect to a messaging pillar?
- [ ] Is it scannable? (headers, bullets, short paragraphs)
